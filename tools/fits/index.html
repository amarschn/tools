Theory

ISO 286 defines standardized tolerance grades and fundamental deviations so that fits can be designed consistently across manufacturing processes.

  • Hole-basis fits keep the hole at an H position while the shaft letter sets the fit class.
  • Clearance fits guarantee positive clearance, transition fits straddle zero, and interference fits are negative.
  • Standard tolerance unit i scales with diameter, producing IT grades for the tolerance width.

Implementation Notes

  • Supported diameters: 1 to 500 mm (ISO diameter steps).
  • Supported IT grades: 5 through 16.
  • Hole letters supported: H, JS.
  • Shaft letters supported: a-h, j/js, k, m, n, p, r, s, t.
  • J fits are approximated as centered (js) zones in this implementation.
  • ANSI/ASME B4.1 fits are mapped to ISO equivalents for sizing guidance.
  • Surface finish guidance is typical; confirm with your manufacturing standard.

References

  • ISO 286-1:2010, Geometrical product specifications (GPS) - ISO code system for tolerances.
  • ISO 286-2:2010, Tables of standard tolerance grades and limit deviations.
