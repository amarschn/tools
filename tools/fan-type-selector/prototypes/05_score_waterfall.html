How the score formula works
Overall suitability score S = w_N · S_N + w_η · S_η + w_U · S_U = 0.58 · S_N + 0.27 · S_η + 0.15 · S_U   (no passage constraint active)
N_s fit — log-space band fit Inside band:   S_N = clamp(1.0 − 0.18 · |ln(ω_s) − ln(ω_opt)| / bandwidth, 0.82, 1.0) Outside band: S_N = clamp(0.72 − 0.45 · outside_distance / bandwidth, 0.08, 0.72)
Efficiency fit S_η = clamp(η_estimated / 0.85, 0.2, 1.0) The 0.85 normalizer is the max typical peak efficiency across all fan families.
Tip-speed fit (piecewise) S_U = 1.0 if U ≤ 20 m/s | 0.9 if ≤ 35 | 0.75 if ≤ 50 | 0.55 if ≤ 65 | 0.55·(65/U) above
Why these weights?
ComponentWeightRationale
Specific-speed fit 0.58 Carries the most weight because fan-family selection is primarily a specific-speed screening problem. The architecture bands are defined around N_s.
Efficiency fit 0.27 Separates credible families after the architecture is physically plausible. Should not override a bad family match.
Tip-speed fit 0.15 Compact proxy for noise and mechanical realism — secondary screen, not the defining criterion.

With a passage constraint active, weights shift: N_s → 0.50, η → 0.22, U → 0.12, passage → 0.16. These are fixed heuristics.

Where do the efficiency estimates come from?
Cordier efficiency correlation (Balje 1981) η = 0.90 + 0.017·ln(ω_s) − 0.059·[ln(ω_s)]² At ω_s = 0.80: η = 0.90 + 0.017·(−0.223) − 0.059·(0.050) = 0.893

This is the theoretical peak total-to-total efficiency along the Cordier line. Each type has its own ceiling (BC: 85%, FC: 65%, Mixed: 84%, Axial: 82%) reflecting typical achievable values. The actual estimate is min(type ceiling, Cordier value) further scaled by the N_s fit factor.

Specific speed and diameter equations
Specific speed ω_s = ω · √Q / (ΔP / ρ)^(3/4) = (2π · 1097/60) · √1.00 / (500 / 1.204)^0.75 = 0.80
Specific diameter (Cordier) ln(δ_s) = 0.833 − 0.524·ln(ω_s) + 0.008·[ln(ω_s)]² Coefficients are a polynomial fit to the Balje 1981 dataset.
Physical wheel diameter D = δ_s · √Q / (ΔP / ρ)^(1/4) = 2.12 · √1.00 / (500/1.204)^0.25 = 0.573 m
