Prototype S: Balje Diagram (Physics-Correct)

This one is built from a single physics model rather than hand-placed ellipses. Every family peak lies on the Cordier line by construction; the "islands" are what emerges when a per-family ηpeak(Ns) ridge is decayed off the Cordier curve. Production-tool candidate.

Model

Every point on the map gets one efficiency value from this formula. The Cordier line and the family humps are not drawn separately — they fall out of the math.

η_peak(N_s) = max_i η_i · exp(−½·[ln(N_s/N_s_i)/σ_i]²)
η(N_s, D_s) = η_peak(N_s) · exp(−β · ln²(D_s/D_s_opt(N_s)))

Ds,opt(Ns) is the tool's existing quadratic Cordier fit. β and each family's (Ns,i, ηi, σi) are the only tunable parameters.

Bottom strip

The lower chart is a horizontal slice through the ridge — peak efficiency along the Cordier line as a function of Ns. This is where the "islands" on the map come from: each family is a hump here.

Honest limits

Physics-consistent, not empirically digitized. Peak-η values and widths are calibrated to the Balje/Dixon envelope — reasonable for screening, not a substitute for measured maps.
