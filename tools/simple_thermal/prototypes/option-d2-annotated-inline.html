Fin & Surface Efficiency
ηf = 91.0%, ηo = 91.7% healthy
▶
Standard 1D fin model (tanh formula). A fin's tip is cooler than its root because heat must conduct along it. Efficiency measures what fraction of the fin area is thermally "useful." At 91%, these fins are working well — below ~70% means you're adding metal for diminishing returns.

Friction & Losses

▶
Three sources of pressure loss: wall friction along the channel (dominant), contraction at channel entry, and expansion at exit. Together they determine how much pressure the airflow must overcome to pass through the heatsink.
